# Task 2: Churn Count Visualization

## Objective

The objective of this task is to visualize the number of churned and non-churned customers using a Column Chart. Customer churn analysis helps organizations understand how many customers have discontinued their services and supports the development of customer retention strategies.

---

## Dataset Information

**Dataset Name:** Telco Customer Churn Dataset

The dataset contains customer information including demographics, account details, service subscriptions, and churn status.

**Target Variable:**

* Churn

  * Yes = Customer left the company
  * No = Customer remained with the company

---

## Problem Statement

Customer churn is one of the most important metrics for subscription-based businesses. Understanding the number of customers who leave compared to those who stay provides valuable insights into customer retention and business performance.

This task focuses on creating a Column Chart to compare churned and non-churned customers.

---

## Tools Used

* Power BI
* Microsoft Excel
* Data Visualization Techniques

---

## Methodology

### Step 1: Load Dataset

The Telco Customer Churn dataset was imported into Power BI.

### Step 2: Analyze Churn Column

The values in the Churn column were categorized into:

* Yes (Churned Customers)
* No (Non-Churned Customers)

### Step 3: Calculate Customer Count

The count of customers for each churn category was calculated.

### Step 4: Create Visualization

A Clustered Column Chart was created with:

* X-Axis: Churn Status
* Y-Axis: Customer Count
* Data Labels: Enabled
* Chart Title: Customer Churn Count

---

## Visualization Results

| Churn Status | Customer Count |
| ------------ | -------------: |
| No           |          5,174 |
| Yes          |          1,869 |

---

## Analysis

### Non-Churned Customers

Total customers retained by the company:

**5,174 Customers**

These customers continue using company services and contribute to recurring revenue.

### Churned Customers

Total customers who discontinued services:

**1,869 Customers**

These customers represent potential revenue loss and indicate areas where customer satisfaction and retention efforts can be improved.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1

The majority of customers have remained with the company.

### Finding 2

Approximately 26.5% of customers have churned.

Calculation:

Churn Rate = (1869 / 7043) × 100

### Finding 3

More than one-fourth of the customer base has left the company, indicating the need for retention strategies.
